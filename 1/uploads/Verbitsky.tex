\begin{abstract} We give a survey of recent results on positive solutions to sublinear elliptic equations of the type
$-Lu+ V \,  u^{q}=f$, where $L$ is an elliptic operator in divergence form, $0<q<1$, $f\geq 0$ and $V$ is a function that may change
sign, in a domain $\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}$, or in a weighted Riemannian
manifold, with a  positive Green's function $G$. We discuss the existence, as well as global lower and upper pointwise estimates of classical and weak solutions $u$, and conditions
that ensure
$u \in L^r(\Omega)$ or $u \in W^{1, p} (\Omega)$.

Some of these results are applicable to homogeneous sublinear integral equations
		$ u = G(u^q d \sigma)$  in $\Omega,$
	where $0<q<1$, and $\sigma=-V$ is a positive  locally finite Borel measure in $\Omega$. Here ${G} (f \, d \sigma)(x) =\int_\Omega G(x, y), \, f(y) \, d \sigma(y)$ is an integral operator with positive  (quasi) symmetric kernel $G$ on $\Omega \times \Omega$ which satisfies the weak maximum principle.
		This includes positive solutions, possibly singular, to sublinear  equations
		 involving the fractional Laplacian,
		\[ (-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}} u = \sigma \, u^q, \quad u \ge 0  \quad \text{in} \, \, \Omega, \]
	where  $0<q<1$, $0 < \alpha < n$ and $u=0$ in $\Omega^c$ and at infinity in domains $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ with positive Green's function $G$.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{sublinear elliptic equations, Green's function, weak maximum principle, fractional Laplacian}{сублинейные эллиптические уравнения, функция Грина, слабый принцип максимума, дробный лапласиан}

\section{Introduction}
	
	Let $\Omega$  be a connected open set in  $ \R^n$,  or a weighted Riemannian
manifold $(M, m)$ (see Sec. \ref{pointwise} below).  We give a survey of recent results on positive solutions $u$ to the sublinear elliptic differential equation%
\begin{equation}\label{uuq}
-Lu+V\left( x\right) u^{q}=f, \quad x \in \Omega,
\end{equation}%
 where $0<q<1$,  and
\begin{equation}
L u= {\rm div} ( \mathcal{A} \nabla u) \label{L}
\end{equation}%
is an  elliptic operator with symmetric  coefficients
$\mathcal{A} = \left( a_{ij} ( x)\right)_{i,j=1}^{n}$ in divergence form, $V$ and $f$ are functions in $\Omega $, and $f\geq 0$. Here $V\left( x\right) $  may change sign, and
no extra  boundary conditions are imposed  on $V$.

One of the main goals is to obtain global pointwise estimates of $u$ in terms of the function
$h$ which is defined as the minimal positive solution in $\Omega $ of the equation $%
-Lh=f,$ where $f \not \equiv 0$.  We also consider positive solutions $u$ to the corresponding homogeneous equation
\begin{equation}\label{f=0}
-Lu+V(x) \,  u^{q}=0,
\end{equation}
with $f \equiv 0$. In Sec.~\ref{pointwise}, we present lower pointwise estimates of solutions
to \eqref{uuq} and \eqref{f=0}
obtained  in \cite{GV1,GV2}.




  There are also analogues of these estimates for positive  (super) solutions $u$ to sublinear integral equations
  \begin{equation}\label{int-eqn}
  u + G(V u^q) = F,
  \end{equation}
which involve the integral operator
  $G f (x) = \int_\Omega G(x, y) \, f(y) \, dy$
    with a general positive kernel $G\colon \Omega\times \Omega \to (0, +\infty]$. If $G=G^\Omega$ is Green's function associated with the elliptic operator $L$ and $F= G f$, then \eqref{int-eqn} is in a sense equivalent to
    \eqref{uuq}.

     In many cases it is enough to  assume that the kernel $G$ is lower semicontinuous,  symmetric (or quasi-symmetric) and satisfies the weak maximum principle (see \cite{GV2}), which includes many non-local operators, for instance, the fractional Laplacian $(-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$. This leads to
     pointwise estimates of solutions for \eqref{int-eqn} in the case where $V=-\sigma\ge 0$, and $\sigma$ is a locally integrable function, or locally finite positive Borel measure in $\Omega$. In particular, we obtain
sharp pointwise estimates of  solutions (possibly singular) and existence results for
     the fractional Laplacian equation
       \begin{equation}
     \label{frac_lap_eqn}
      (-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}} u =  \sigma \, u^q, \quad u \ge 0  \quad \text{in} \, \, \Omega, \quad u=0
       \quad \text{in} \, \, \Omega^c,
      \end{equation}
     in general domains $\Omega\subset \R^n$ with positive Green's function $G$ for $0<\alpha\le 2$, or nice domains (the entire space $\R^n$, or balls, or half-spaces in $\R^n$) for $0<\alpha<n$.

    For entire solutions on $\Omega=\R^n$ and $V=-\sigma\ge 0$, where $\sigma$ is a locally finite positive Borel measure in $\R^n$, there are more complicated matching upper and lower  bounds given in terms of certain nonlinear potentials of Th. Wolff type \cite{CV1,CV2,CV3}. Earlier pointwise
estimates for \textit{bounded} positive solutions are due to Brezis and Kamin \cite{BK}.  We consider such results in Sec.~\ref{upper}.





    In the case $V=-\sigma\ge 0$, there are necessary and sufficient conditions for the existence of positive solutions $u \in L^q (\Omega, \sigma)$ and
    $u \in L^q_{{\rm loc}} (\Omega, \sigma)$ to \eqref{int-eqn}. The latter class includes all positive solutions $0<u<+\infty$ $d \sigma$-a.e., and can be characterized either using
    certain localization techniques developed in \cite{CV2} in the case $ L=(-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$ and $\Omega=\R^n$, or integral inequalities with weights as in  \cite{QV2} in the general case. These results are discussed in Sec.~\ref{upper} and Sec.~\ref{existence}.

       We also present in Sec. \ref{higher}
       criteria for the existence of positive solutions to   \eqref{int-eqn} of higher
    integrability $u \in L^{q+\gamma} (\Omega, \sigma)$
    ($\gamma>0$), which are easier to characterize and are useful in applications (see \cite{V}). In particular, the case $\gamma=1$, i.e., solutions $u \in L^{1+q}(\Omega, \sigma)$, corresponds to finite energy solutions to sublinear elliptic equations \eqref{f=0}
    in the Dirichlet space $u \in W^{1, 2}(\Omega)$ for uniformly elliptic operators $L$ in  divergence form.

    Similar criteria for $\gamma>0$ can be applied to deduce conditions on $\sigma$ which ensure the existence of positive solutions $u$ to \eqref{f=0}
    in Sobolev spaces $W^{1, p}(\Omega)$ and
    Lebesgue spaces $L^r(\Omega, dx)$ for general domains $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ with positive Green's functions (see \cite{QV1,QV3}). Some earlier existence results of this type for $\sigma \in L^s(\Omega, dx)$
     in bounded domains $\Omega$ are due to Boccardo and Orsina \cite{BO,BO2}. Such existence theorems  under more general assumptions on $\sigma$ which include measures (possibly singular with respect to Lebesgue measure) are discussed in Sec.~\ref{Lpsolutions}.


\section{Lower pointwise estimates of supersolutions}\label{pointwise}

In this section, we assume that  $\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}$ is a connected open set, and consider the divergence form  operator
\begin{equation}
Lu= {\rm div} (\mathcal{A} \nabla u)\left( x\right)= \sum_{i,j=1}^{n}\partial _{x_{i}}\left( a_{ij}\left(
x\right) \partial _{x_{j}}u\right) ,  \label{Lba}
\end{equation}%
where $a_{ij}$ are $C^2$ functions in $\Omega$,  and $a_{ij}=a_{ji}$. Assume
that $L$ is elliptic, that is, the matrix $\mathcal{A}=\left( a_{ij}\left( x\right)
\right) $ is positive definite for any $x$ (the uniform ellipticity is not
required).




Let us assume that the Dirichlet Green function of $L$ in $\Omega $ exists. We
denote it by $G^{\Omega }\left( x,y\right) $. We set
\begin{equation*}
h\left( x\right) = Gf(x)=\int_{\Omega }G^{\Omega }\left( x,y\right) f\left(
y\right) dy,
\end{equation*}%
where $ f \ge 0$ ($f \not\equiv 0$), and assume that $h\left( x\right) <\infty $ for all $x\in \Omega $ (note
also that $h\left( x\right) >0$ in $\Omega $), and that the integral%
\begin{equation}
G(h^q V)(x)=\int_{\Omega }G^{\Omega }\left( x,y\right) h^{q}\left( y\right) V\left(
y\right) dy  \label{Gh}
\end{equation}%
is well-defined.

An interesting special case is when $f\equiv 1$, and $h$ is the solution of the Dirichlet
problem:
\begin{equation}
\begin{cases}
-L h=1\quad \text{in}\,\,\Omega , \\
h=0\quad \text{on}\,\,\partial \Omega.
\end{cases}
\label{exit-time}
\end{equation}%
In other words, $h(x)=\mathbb{E}_{x}[\tau _{\Omega }]$, where $\tau _{\Omega
}=\inf \{t\!:\,X_{t}\not\in \Omega \}$ is the first exit time from $\Omega $
of the (rescaled) Brownian motion $X_{t}$, and $x\in \Omega $ is a starting
point. For bounded $C^{1,1}$ domains $\Omega$, it is well known that $h(x)\simeq d_{\Omega }(x)$, where
\begin{equation}
d_{\Omega }(x)=\text{dist}(x,\partial \Omega ).  \label{dOm}
\end{equation}%



One of the main results  of \cite{GV1} in the case  $0<q<1$
is the following global lower
estimate for a  (super) solution $u>0$ to \eqref{uuq}.

\begin{theorem}
\label{main-thm2}\label{T1} Let $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ be a domain with a positive
Green function $G^{\Omega }$. Suppose $V, \, f\in C(\Omega )$, and  $f\geq
0,$ $f\not\equiv 0$ in $\Omega $. Let $u\in C^{2}(\Omega )$ satisfy the inequality
\begin{equation}\label{semilinear2A}
- L u+Vu^{q}\geq f, \quad  u> 0 \, \, \, \text{in} \, \, \, \Omega.
\end{equation}%
 Set $h=G f$ and assume that $h<\infty $ in $\Omega $. Assume also
that $G^{\Omega }(h^{q}V)(x)$  is well-defined for all $x\in \Omega $. Then the
following estimate holds for all $x\in \Omega:$
\begin{equation}
u(x)\geq h(x)\left[ 1-(1-q)\frac{G (h^{q}V)(x)}{h(x)}%
\right] _{+}^{\frac{1}{1-q}}.  \label{main}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

This theorem is actually proved in \cite{GV1} in a more general  context of elliptic operators on
weighted manifolds $\left( M, m\right) $. We recall that if $M$ is a smooth Riemannian manifold with the Riemannian
metric tensor $g=\left( g_{ij}\right) $, then the associated Laplace --- Beltrami
operator $\mathcal{L}_{0}$ acts on $C^{2}$ functions $u$ on $M$ and is given
in any chart $x_{1},...,x_{n}$ by
\begin{equation*}
\mathcal{L}_{0}u=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\det g}}\sum_{i,j=1}^{n}\partial
_{x_{i}}\left( \sqrt{\det g}g^{ij}\partial _{x_{j}}u\right).
\end{equation*}%
Here $\det g$ is the determinant of the matrix $g=\left( g_{ij}\right) $,
and $\left( g^{ij}\right) $ is the inverse matrix of $\left( g_{ij}\right) $. The Riemannian measure $m_{0}$ is given in the same chart by%
\begin{equation*}
dm_{0}=\sqrt{\det g}dx_{1}...dx_{n},
\end{equation*}%
so that $\mathcal{L}_{0}$ is symmetric with respect to $m_{0}.$ Using the
gradient operator $\nabla $ defined by
\begin{equation*}
\left( \nabla u\right) ^{i}=\sum_{j=1}^{n}g^{ij}\partial _{x_{j}}u
\end{equation*}%
and the divergence ${\rm div}$ on vector fields $F^{i}$%
\begin{equation*}
{\rm div}F=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\det g}}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\partial _{x_{i}}\left(
\sqrt{\det g}F^{i}\right) ,
\end{equation*}%
we have
\begin{equation*}
\mathcal{L}_{0}={\rm div}\circ \nabla .
\end{equation*}


Now let $\omega $ be a smooth positive function on $M$ and consider the measure $%
m$ on $M$ given by
\begin{equation*}
dm=\omega dm_{0}.
\end{equation*}%
The couple $\left( M, m\right) $ is called a weighted manifold, and $\omega $ in this context
 is called a weight  (see \cite{G}). The
following operator $\mathcal{L}$
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}u:=\frac{1}{\omega }{\rm div}\left( \omega \nabla u\right) =%
\frac{1}{\omega \sqrt{\det g}}\sum_{i,j=1}^{n}\partial _{x_{i}}\left( \omega
\sqrt{\det g}g^{ij}\partial _{x_{j}}u\right)  \label{Lw}
\end{equation}%
acting on $C^{2}$ functions $u$ on $M$, is called the (weighted) Laplace
operator of $\left( M, m\right) $. This operator $\mathcal{L}$, which is symmetric with respect to measure $m$,
 can be  used in place of the operator $L$ in  $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ above.


 There is also a similar lower estimate for positive (super) solutions to the homogeneous equation \eqref{f=0}.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\begin{theorem}
\label{main-thm3}\label{T3} Let  $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ be a domain with a positive Green
function $G^{\Omega }$. Suppose $V\in C(\Omega )$, and  $u\in C^{2}(\Omega )$
satisfies the inequality
\begin{equation}\label{diff2}
- L u+V\,u^{q}\geq 0,\quad u\geq 0 \,\, \, \text{in} \,\,\, \Omega,
\end{equation}
where  $0<q<1$.
Assume also that $G V (x)$  is well-defined for all $x\in \Omega $.



Then  the
following estimate holds for all $x \in \Omega:$
\begin{equation}
u(x)\geq \left[ -(1-q)\,G V (x)\right] _{+}^{\frac{1}{1-q}%
}.  \label{est3-thm3}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}




Similar global pointwise estimates are obtained in \cite{GV1} for \textit{all} exponents  $q \in \R$ ($q \not=0$), but we do  not consider the case $q \not\in (0, 1)$ here. Also, the preceding estimate remains true for {\it nonnegative} solutions $u$
if the integration in the Green's potentials $G^{\Omega }(h^{q}V)$ and  $G^{\Omega } V$ respectively is taken over the set $\Omega^{+}=\{ x \in \Omega\colon u>0\}$ in place of $\Omega$. Note that the  differential inequalities \eqref{semilinear2A} and \eqref{diff2} in Theorem \ref{main-thm2} and Theorem \ref{main-thm3} respectively are understood in the classical sense.






%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%


In the case $V=-\sigma \le 0$, where $\sigma$ is a  locally finite measure in $\Omega$, the preceding estimate yields
\begin{equation}\label{h-pos}
u(x)\geq \left[ (1-q) \int_{\Omega}G^{\Omega} (x, y)   d \sigma (y)\right] ^{\frac{1}{1-q}},
\end{equation}
which  holds for all non-trivial weak solutions $u$ (possibly singular).

This estimate was obtained for the Laplacian $L =\Delta$ in the case $\Omega=\R^n$ in \cite{BK}, without specifying the sharp constant $(1-q)^{\frac{1}{1-q}}$,
  under some additional assumptions on $\sigma\ge 0$ (see also \cite{CV3}).

  There are analogues of this estimate for integral operators with general positive kernels $G\colon \Omega\times \Omega \to (0, +\infty]$
  on a locally compact Hausdorff space $\Omega$ that are lower semicontinuous, symmetric,  and satisfy the weak maximum principle
  with constant $\mathfrak{b}\ge 1$ (see \cite{GV2}).

   For a locally finite Radon measure $\nu \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$, we denote by $G \nu$ the  potential
  \[
  {G}\nu (x) = \int_\Omega G(x, y) \, d \nu(y).
  \]

  We recall that the  kernel $G$
	is said to satisfy the \textit{weak maximum principle} (with constant $ \mathfrak{b}\ge 1$) provided that
		\begin{equation}\label{weak-max}
		{G}\nu (x) \le M, \quad \forall x \in {\rm supp} \, \nu \,\, \Longrightarrow \, \,  {G}\nu (x) \le \mathfrak{b} \, M, \quad \forall x \in \Omega,
		\end{equation}
		for any constant $M>0$ and $\nu \in \mathcal{M}^+(\Omega)$. When $\mathfrak{b}=1$, we say that ${G}$ satisfies the \textit{strong maximum principle}.





  These assumptions hold for many non-local operators
  $L$ of interest in nonlinear analysis, in particular, the fractional Laplacian operators $(-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$ in domains $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$. For such kernels $G$ and $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$, let
  \[
  G (f \, d \sigma)(x) = \int_\Omega G(x, y) \, f(y) \, d \sigma(y),  \quad x \in \Omega,
  \]
  where $f\ge 0$ is a measurable function.



  If $u>0$ $d\sigma$-a.e. is a supersolution, i.e.,
\begin{equation} \label{supersol2}
G(u^q d \sigma) (x) \le u(x) < \infty \quad d \sigma{\rm-a.e.},
\end{equation}
then
\begin{equation} \label{lowerest2}
u (x) \ge  (1-q)^{\frac{1}{1-q}} \mathfrak{b}^{-\frac{q}{1-q}}   \Big[ G \sigma (x)\Big]^{\frac{1}{1-q}} \quad d \sigma{\rm-a.e.} \, \, {\rm in} \,\, \Omega.
\end{equation}
The constant  $(1-q)^{\frac{1}{1-q}}$ in  \eqref{lowerest2} in the case $\mathfrak{b}=1$ coincides with that in \eqref{h-pos}. Similar results hold for \textit{quasi-symmetric}  kernels $G$
such that there exists a constant $\mathfrak{a} >0$ for which
\begin{equation}\label{quasi}
G(x, y) \le \mathfrak{a} \, G(x, y), \quad x, y \in \Omega,
		\end{equation}
with a constant on the right-hand side of \eqref{lowerest2} depending on both $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{b}$. See \cite{An} where quasi-symmetry
is established for many elliptic operators in non-divergence form. Properties \eqref{weak-max} and
\eqref{quasi} will be used extensively in Sections \ref{existence} and \ref{Lpsolutions} below.

  \section{Matching upper and lower pointwise estimates of solutions}\label{upper}

  In this section, we discuss recent results established in \cite{CV2} for entire solutions to quasilinear
  elliptic equations of the type
\begin{equation}\label{eq1}
\left\{ \begin{array}{ll}
-\Delta_pu=\sigma \, u^q & \text{in} \, \, \,  {\R}^n,\\&\\
\displaystyle{\liminf_{x \to \infty} \, u(x)}=0, & \, \, u>0,
\end{array} \right.
\end{equation}
where  $0<q<p-1$. Here $\Delta_p =\text{div}(\nabla u|\nabla u|^{p-2})$ is the $p$-Laplace operator,
and $\sigma\ge 0$ is an arbitrary  locally integrable function, or locally finite Borel measure, $\sigma \in \M$; if $\sigma \in L^1_{{\rm loc}} (\R^n)$ we write $ d \sigma= \sigma \, dx$.


The main goal  is to give necessary and sufficient conditions on $\sigma$ for the existence of weak solutions to \eqref{eq1},  and obtain matching upper and lower global pointwise bounds for positive solutions.  In this approach,  key integral
inequalities are identified, and  new nonlinear potentials of Wolff type are constructed that are intrinsic
to a number of similar problems.




This approach is applicable to general quasilinear $\mathcal{A}$-Laplace operators of divergence type $\text{div} \,  \mathcal{A}(x, \nabla u)$ under standard boundedness and structural assumptions on $\mathcal{A}$, as well as to fully nonlinear $k$-Hessian operators,
and the fractional Laplacian equations (see \cite{CV2}).



We will restrict ourselves here to the case $p=2$ and linear operators $\text{div} ( \mathcal{A} \nabla u)$
under the  uniform ellipticity assumption on the  matrix $ \mathcal{A} \in L^\infty(\R^n)^{n \times n}$. In this setup, the fundamental solution of the $\mathcal{A}$-Laplacian is known to be pointwise equivalent to that of the Laplacian, which reduces the problem to estimates involving Newtonian potentials.

 In fact, we will consider below the fractional Laplacian equations,
\begin{equation}\label{fraclap}
\left\{ \begin{array}{ll}
(-\Delta )^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}u=\sigma \, u^q & \text{in} \, \, \,  \mathbb{R}^n,\\ &\\
\displaystyle{\liminf_{ x \to \infty} \, u(x)}=0,& \, \, u>0,
\end{array} \right.
\end{equation}
for $0<q<1$ and $0<\alpha < n$; this includes the range $\alpha>2$ where the usual maximum principle is not available.




In the classical case $p=2$,  equation \eqref{eq1}, or equivalently \eqref{fraclap} with $\alpha=2$,
provides a model {\it sublinear} elliptic problem if $0<q<1$. It is easy to see that it is equivalent to the integral equation $u =N (u^q d \sigma)$, where
$N \omega=(-\Delta)^{-1} \omega$ is the Newtonian potential of  $d \omega = u^q d \sigma$ on $\R^n$.


As we emphasize in \cite{CV2},  such problems are governed by the important   integral inequality
\begin{equation}\label{weight-endpoint}
\left(\int_{\R^n} |\varphi|^q \, d \sigma\right)^{\frac 1 q} \le \varkappa \,  \Vert \Delta \varphi \Vert_{L^1(\R^n)},
\end{equation}
for all test functions $\varphi \in C^2(\R^n)$ vanishing at infinity such that $-\Delta \varphi \ge 0$. This inequality turns out to be necessary and sufficient for the existence of a positive solution
$u \in L^q(\R^n, \sigma)$ to  \eqref{eq1} if $p=2$ and $0<q<1$.

Inequality \eqref{weight-endpoint} represents the end-point case of the well-studied $(L^p, L^q)$ trace inequalities for $p>1$. A comprehensive  treatment
of trace inequalities in that case in terms of Sobolev capacities can be found  in \cite{Maz}.

To characterize the existence of arbitrary weak solutions $u \in L^q_{{\rm loc}} (\R^n, \sigma)$, and obtain sharp global pointwise estimates, a localization procedure
is used in \cite{CV2} which involves the so-called intrinsic Wolff potentials.

More precisely, one needs to consider a localized version of \eqref{weight-endpoint} where the measure
$\sigma$ is replaced with $\sigma_B=\chi_B \, \sigma$ restricted to a
ball $B=B(x, r)$, and the corresponding best constant $\varkappa$ is denoted by $\varkappa(B)$. These constants are used as building blocks in the key tool, a  potential of Th. Wolff type (see \cite{AH} for the  theory and history of Wolff's potentials),
\begin{equation} \label{potential}
K \sigma (x)  =  \int_0^{\infty} \frac{ \left[\varkappa(B(x, r))\right]^{\frac{q}{1-q}}}{r^{n-2}}\frac{dr}{r},  \quad x \in \R^n.
\end{equation}
This nonlinear potential, together with the usual Newtonian potential $N \sigma$, provides sharp bilateral estimates of solutions in the case $p=2$ and  $0<q<1$.





This work has been motivated by the results of  Brezis and Kamin \cite{BK} who proved that \eqref{eq1},  with $p=2$ and $0<q<1$, has a {\it bounded} solution $u$ on $\R^n$ if and only
if  ${\bf N} \sigma \in L^{\infty}(\R^n)$; moreover,  such a solution is unique, and there exists a constant $c>0$ so that
\begin{equation} \label{b-k}
c^{-1} \, \Big ( N \sigma(x) \Big)^{\frac{1}{1-q}} \le u (x) \le c \, N \sigma (x), \quad x \in \R^n.
\end{equation}

The lower bound in \eqref{b-k}, which is  generalized in \eqref{h-pos} and
\eqref{lowerest2}, holds for all solutions. On the other hand,  the upper bound is valid only for bounded solutions. Some
generalizations of the upper bound to possibly singular solutions
in the form
\[
 u (x) \le c \, \left[ N \sigma (x) + \Big ( N \sigma(x)\Big)^{\frac{1}{1-q}}\right], \quad x \in \R^n,\]
under additional assumptions on $\sigma$, can be found in \cite{CV3}.


As was pointed out in  \cite{BK}, both the lower and upper estimates  in \eqref{b-k} are sharp in a sense.  However, there is a substantial gap between them. In Theorem \ref{mainthm} below
this gap is bridged by using   $K \sigma + (N \sigma)^{\frac{1}{1-q}} $. The corresponding pointwise  estimates hold for all,  possibly singular solutions, as well
as more general nonlinear equations.


In the context of fractional Laplacian equations \eqref{fraclap}, we denote by  $I_{\alpha}$
 the Riesz potential of order $ \alpha\in (0, n)$ on $\R^n$:
$$I_{\alpha}\mu= |x|^{\alpha-n} \star \mu =  c(\alpha, n) \, (-\Delta)^{-\frac{\alpha}{2}} \mu,$$
for $\mu \in \M$. In what follows,  the normalization
constant $c(\alpha, n)$ will be dropped for the sake of convenience; in particular,
$I_2 \mu=N \mu$ stands for the Newtonian potential.



We now introduce  some elements of nonlinear potential theory which is intrinsic to \eqref{fraclap}. Let $B=B(x, r)=\{y \in \R^n: \, |x-y|<r\}$ be a ball in $\R^n$, and
let   $\sigma_B=\chi_B \, \sigma$ stand for the measure $\sigma$   restricted to  $B$.
By
$\kappa(B)$ we denote  the least constant in the localized integral inequality
\begin{equation} \label{Rieszineq}
\Vert I_{\alpha} \nu \Vert_{L^q(d\sigma_{B})} \le \kappa(B) \, \nu(\R^n), \quad \forall \nu \in \M,
\end{equation}
where $0<q<1$ and $0<\alpha<n$.

We observe
that the constant $\kappa(B)$ does not change if we restrict ourselves to absolutely
continuous $\nu \in L^1_{+}(\R^n)$ on the right-hand side of \eqref{Rieszineq}.




We define the corresponding nonlinear potential of Wolff type by
\begin{equation} \label{potential-frac}
K_{\alpha, q} \sigma (x)  =  \int_0^{\infty} \frac{\left[ \kappa(B(x, r))\right]^{\frac{q}{1-q}}}{r^{n- \alpha}}\frac{dr}{r},  \quad x \in \R^n.
\end{equation}


The following condition  ensures that both $K_{\alpha, q} \sigma$ and $I_{ \alpha} \sigma$  are not identically infinite:
\begin{equation}\label{Rieszfinite}
 \int_1^{\infty} \frac{ \left[\kappa(B(0, r))\right]^{\frac{q}{1-q}}}{r^{n- \alpha }}\frac{dr}{r}  + \int_1^{\infty} \frac{\sigma(B(0, r))}{r^{n-  \alpha}}\frac{dr}{r} < \infty,
\end{equation}

 Clearly, the first part of  \eqref{Rieszfinite} is equivalent to $K_{\alpha, q} \sigma \not\equiv +\infty$, whereas
 the second one is equivalent to  $I_\alpha \sigma\not\equiv +\infty$ (and  $I_\alpha \sigma< +\infty$ a.e.)


 Note that solutions $u \in L^q_{{\rm loc}} (\R^n, d \sigma)$ to \eqref{fraclap} are understood in the weak sense.
\begin{theorem} \label{mainthm}
Let  $0 < q < 1$, $0<\alpha<n$, and  $\sigma \in \M$.

{\rm (i)} If \eqref{Rieszfinite} holds, then  there exists a minimal  solution $u>0$ to \eqref{fraclap} such that
\begin{equation} \label{two-sided}
c^{-1}   \left [K_{\alpha, q}  \sigma + \left(I_\alpha \sigma\right)^{\frac{1}{1-q}}\right]
\le u \le c  \left [K_{\alpha, q}  \sigma + \left(I_\alpha \sigma\right)^{\frac{1}{1-q}}\right],
\end{equation}
where $c>0$ is a constant which  depends only on  $q$, $\alpha$, and $n$.


{\rm (ii)} Conversely, if there exists a nontrivial   (super) solution $v$ to \eqref{fraclap}, then \eqref{Rieszfinite} holds,  and $v \ge u$, where $u$ is the minimal solution of statement {\rm (i)}.
\end{theorem}

We observe that neither of the two parts of the condition  \eqref{Rieszfinite} implies the other one.
Condition  $I_\alpha \sigma<\infty$ a.e. alone is not enough to ensure the existence of a global solution $u$ even if all
the local embedding constants $\kappa(B)$ in \eqref{Rieszineq} are finite,  unless $\sigma$ is radially symmetric (see \cite{CV3}).



  \section{Existence of solutions for sublinear integral equations}\label{existence}

  	In this section,  $G \colon \Omega\times \Omega \to (0, +\infty]$ is a lower semicontinuous,  quasi-symmetric kernel on $\Omega\times \Omega$  that satisfies \eqref{quasi}
  along with the weak maximum principle \eqref{weak-max}, on a locally compact Hausdorff space $\Omega$.
  Let us fix a locally finite Radon measure $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$.

   In \cite{QV1,QV2,V}, necessary and sufficient conditions are obtained for the existence of positive (super) solutions to the sublinear integral equation
   \begin{equation}
			\label{sol}
			u =  G(u^q d \sigma), \quad 0<u<+\infty \quad d \sigma-\text{a.e.}  \, \,  \text{ in $\Omega$},
				\end{equation}

  The existence of solutions in Lebesgue spaces, $u \in L^\gamma (\Omega, \sigma)$,   turned out to be related to weighted norm inequalities of the type
  \begin{equation}\label{weight-sigma}
\Vert G (f \, d \sigma)\Vert_{L^r(\Omega, d \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma)}, \quad \forall f \in L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma),
\end{equation}
for integral operators $G$  in the case $0<r<s<+\infty$, $s \ge 1$.

The most important and most difficult case is when $r=q$ ($0<r<1$) and $s=1$, i.e.,
 \begin{equation}\label{weight-sigma-1}
\Vert G (f \, d \sigma)\Vert_{L^q(\Omega, d \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^{1} (\Omega, d \sigma)}, \quad \forall f \in L^{1} (\Omega, d \sigma).
\end{equation}



 In the special case of Green's kernels and the corresponding elliptic operators $L$,
 \eqref{weight-sigma-1}  boils down to a non-standard  trace inequality
 \begin{equation}\label{weight-non}
\Vert \phi \Vert_{L^q(\Omega, d \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert L \phi \Vert_{L^{1} (\Omega, d x)}, \quad \forall  \,
 \phi \in C^2_0(\overline{\Omega}), \, \, \, L \phi  \ge 0.
\end{equation}


It will be more convenient, as it often happens in the end-point case $p=1$, to replace \eqref{weight-sigma-1}  with a similar inequality for measures
$\nu \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$ in place of $L^{1} (\Omega, d \sigma)$,
 \begin{equation}\label{weight-meas}
\Vert G  \nu\Vert_{L^q(\Omega, d \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert \nu \Vert, \quad \forall  \nu \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega),
\end{equation}
where $\Vert \nu \Vert = \nu(\Omega)$.
Clearly, \eqref{weight-meas}$\Longrightarrow$\eqref{weight-sigma-1} if we set $d \nu = |f| \, d \sigma$. The converse is also true in many instances.


The following theorem demonstrates that a positive  solution $u \in L^q(\Omega, \sigma)$ to \eqref{sol}
exists if and only if \eqref{weight-meas} holds. The same condition characterizes the existence of
a positive supersolution $u\in L^q(\Omega, \sigma)$ such that
\begin{equation}
\label{super-sol}
			u \ge  G(u^q \sigma), \quad 0<u<+\infty \quad d \sigma-\text{a.e.}  \, \,  \text{ in $\Omega$}.
				\end{equation}



	\begin{theorem}\label{strong-thm}
		Let $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$ and $0<q<1$.  Suppose $G$ is a  lower semicontinuous, quasi-symmetric kernel which satisfies the weak maximum principle.
		Then the following statements are equivalent:
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item There exists a positive constant $C=C(\sigma, G)$ such that \eqref{weight-meas} holds.
			\item There exists a supersolution $u \in L^q(\Omega, d \sigma)$ such that \eqref{super-sol}  holds.
			\item There exists a solution $u\in L^q(\Omega, d \sigma)$ to \eqref{sol}.
		\end{enumerate}
	\end{theorem}
	
	This theorem is proved in \cite{QV2} for more general \textit{non-negative} kernels under the additional
	\textit{non-degeneracy} assumption on the kernel $G$, which is in fact necessary for the existence of
	a positive solution. Non-degeneracy of $G$ is not needed for the existence of a non-trivial positive super-solution.


The next theorem gives some necessary and also close sufficient conditions for  \eqref{weight-meas} to hold.
	
	\begin{theorem}
		\label{energy_norm_results}
		Let $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$, and $0<q<1$.  Suppose $G$ is a quasi-symmetric, non-degenerate kernel which satisfies the weak maximum principle.
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item If   \eqref{weight-meas} holds, then ${G}\sigma \in L^{\frac{q}{1-q}}(\Omega, \sigma)$.
			\item If ${G}\sigma \in L^{\frac{q}{1-q}, 1}(\Omega, \sigma)$, then  \eqref{weight-meas} holds.
		\end{enumerate}
	\end{theorem}
	Here $L^{s, 1}(\Omega, \sigma)$ is the corresponding Lorentz space (see \cite{SW}).
	
	
	It is shown in \cite{QV2} that, without
	the assumption that $G$ satisfies the weak maximum principle, the condition
	\begin{equation}	
	\label{energy-s}
	 \int_{\Omega}  ({G}\sigma)^s  d \sigma < \infty
	\end{equation}
	with $s=\frac{q}{1-q}$ is necessary for the existence of a (super)solution $u \in L^q(\Omega, \sigma)$ to \eqref{sol}  only if $q\in (0, q_0]$, where
	 \[q_0 = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}=0.61\ldots\]
	 denotes the conjugate golden ratio.  For general measures $\sigma$,
	the existence of a positive  solution $u \in L^q(\Omega, \sigma)$ does not guarantee that
	\eqref{energy-s} holds if  $q \in (q_0, 1)$, or $s \not=\frac{q}{1-q}$ for all $q\in(0, 1)$, even for symmetric positive kernels $G$.
	
	
	Another characterization of the strong type inequality \eqref{weight-meas} can be deduced from Maurey's results \cite{M}: it  is  equivalent to the existence of a nonnegative function $F \in L^1(\Omega, \sigma)$ which satisfies
		\[ \sup_{y \in \Omega} \int_\Omega  G(x,y) \, F(x)^{1-\frac{1}{q}} \, d\sigma(x) < +\infty. \]
		This is a dual reformulation of  \eqref{weight-meas}, which does not
		require $G$ to satisfy the weak maximum principle. In the discrete case where $\Omega$ consists
		of a finite number of points, it represents the duality of the two basic concave programming problems
		(see \cite[Sec. 5.7]{BG}).
		
			
	

	
	 We also give in \cite{QV1,QV2,V} explicit characterizations of  the weak type $(1, q)$-inequality
		\begin{equation}
			\label{main_weak_inequality}
			\Vert {G}\nu \Vert_{L^{q,\infty} (\Omega, d\sigma)} \le C \Vert \nu \Vert, \quad \text{ for all } \nu \in \mathcal{M}^+(\Omega),
		\end{equation}
	for any $q>0$, in terms of energy estimates, as well as capacities.
		

	
	In \cite{QV2}, we demonstrate how to remove the extra assumption imposed in
	Theorem~\ref{strong-thm}
	that a (super)solution $u \in L^q(\Omega,  \sigma)$ globally. We prove
	the following theorem where we only assume that $u \in L^q_{{\rm loc}} (\sigma)$, or equivalently,
	$0<u<+\infty$ $d \sigma$-a.e., provided the kernel $G$ satisfies a weak  form of the \textit{complete
	maximum principle}, or alternatively if $G$ is a quasi-metric kernel (see, e.g.,  \cite{H,FNV,QV2}).
	
	The weak form (with constant $\mathfrak{b} \ge 1$) of the complete comparison principle, also called the \textit{domination principle}, says the following:
	\[{G}\mu(x) \le v(x)\, \,   \text{on} \, \, {\rm supp} \, (\mu),  \, \, {G}\mu < + \infty \, \, d \mu-\text{a.e.} \Longrightarrow {G} \mu(x) \le \mathfrak{b} \, v(x) \, \,  \text{on}  \, \, \Omega,
	\] for any positive measure $\mu$ and positive superharmonic function $v$.
	
	
	

		With a special function $m$ satisfying $0 < m < +\infty$ $d\sigma$-a.e., known as a \textit{modifier} \cite{H,FNV}, we can modify the kernel $G$, so that the modified kernel
		\begin{equation}
		\label{mod-ker}
		K(x, y) = \frac{G(x, y)}{m(x) \, m(y)}, \quad x, y \in \Omega,
		\end{equation}
		satisfies the weak maximum principle \eqref{weak-max}. This makes it possible to apply Theorem~\ref{strong-thm}
		with $K$ in place of $G$, and consider $u \in L^q_{{\rm loc}} (\Omega, \sigma)$. A typical modifier
		that works for general  kernels $G$ which satisfy the complete
	maximum principle is given by
	\begin{equation}
		\label{typ-mod}
m(x) = \min \{1, G(x, x_0)\}, \quad x \in \Omega,
	\end{equation}
	where $x_0$ is a fixed pole in $\Omega$ \cite{HN}.

	
		We say that $G$ is a \textit{quasimetric} kernel with quasimetric constant $\kappa>0$ provided $G$ is symmetric and $d(x,y)\,:=\,\frac{1}{G(x,y)}$ satisfies the quasimetric triangle inequality with quasimetric constant $\kappa$, i.e.,
		\begin{equation}\label{quasitr}
			d(x,y) \le \kappa [d(x,z) + d(z, y)],
		\end{equation}
		for any $x, y, z \in \Omega$. Here  $d(x,y)\colon \,  \Omega \times \Omega \rightarrow [0, + \infty)$.
	
	The kernel $G$ is \textit{quasimetrically modifiable} with constant $\kappa$ if there exists a modifier  $m$  such that
		\begin{equation}
			K(x,y) = \frac{G(x,y)}{m(x)m(y)}
		\end{equation}
		is a quasimetric kernel with quasimetric constant $\kappa$.


	
	\begin{theorem}
		\label{local_solutions-Intro}
		Let $\sigma \in \mplus{\Omega}$ and $0 < q < 1$.
		Suppose $G$ is a quasi-symmetric positive kernel, continuous in the extended sense on $\Omega\times\Omega$, which either (a) satisfies the complete maximum principle, or (b) is quasi-metrically modifiable. Then the following statements are equivalent:
		\begin{enumerate}
			\item There exists a positive constant $C$ such that the weighted norm inequality
			\begin{equation}
		\label{weight-G}
		\Vert G \nu \Vert_{L^q(m \, d\sigma)} \le C \,  \int_\Omega m \, d\nu, \quad \text{for all } \nu \in \mplus{\Omega},
	\end{equation}
			holds, where the modifier $m(x)$ is given by \eqref{typ-mod} for some $x_0\in \Omega$. \medskip
			
			\item There exists a positive (super) solution $u$   to \eqref{sol} such that $u \in L^q_{\rm loc}(\Omega, \sigma)$ (or equivalently $0<u<+\infty$ $d\sigma$-a.e.)
		\end{enumerate}
		\end{theorem}

	
	Theorem~\ref{local_solutions-Intro} yields a characterization of the existence of weak solutions
	$u \in L^q_{\rm loc}(\Omega, \sigma)$
	to  the fractional Laplacian equation \eqref{frac_lap_eqn} in general domains $\Omega$ with positive Green's function $G$ for $0<\alpha\le 2$, or the entire space $\R^n$, or balls, or half-spaces in $\R^n$, for $0<\alpha<n$ (quasi-metric kernels $G$)
	as discussed in the introduction.  In the classical case $\alpha=2$,
	 such solutions are the so-called very weak solutions for bounded $C^2$-domains $\Omega$ (see, e.g., \cite{MV}).
	
	 In the case $\Omega=\R^n$, an alternative criterion for the existence of weak solutions
	$u \in L^q_{\rm loc}(\R^n, \sigma)$
	to  the fractional Laplacian equation \eqref{fraclap} based on localization of \eqref{weight-meas}
	is discussed in Sec. \ref{upper} above.


	
  \section{Higher integrability of solutions}\label{higher}
	
	Under the assumptions on the kernel $G$ imposed at the beginning of Sec.~\ref{existence},
	let us consider conditions on $\sigma$ which ensure the existence of solutions
	$u \in L^{r}(\Omega, \sigma)$ with $r>0$ to the sublinear integral equation \eqref{sol}. In this case,  a simple necessary
	and sufficient condition in terms of the energy \eqref{energy-s} was obtained in \cite{V}. In particular, these results were used in \cite{QV3}
	to establish conditions for the existence of  solutions $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$
	(now with respect to Lebesgue measure) to the fractional Laplacian equation \eqref{frac_lap_eqn} in a general domain $\Omega$ with positive Green's function $G$, and also $W^{1, p} (\Omega)$ solutions to
	divergence form sublinear elliptic equations \eqref{f=0}. These results are discussed in Sec.~\ref{Lpsolutions}
	below.
	
	The following theorem (see \cite{V}) characterizes  the existence of solutions $u \in L^{r}(\Omega, \sigma)$ for $r>q$, along with related weighted norm inequalities
	\begin{equation}\label{strong-type}
		\Vert {G} (f d\sigma) \Vert_{L^r(\Omega, \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^p(\Omega, \sigma)},
		\quad \forall f \in L^p(\Omega, \sigma).
	\end{equation}

	
	
	\begin{theorem}\label{thm-a}
		Let $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$.
		 Suppose $G$ is a positive, lower semicontinuous, quasi-symmetric kernel on $\Omega$ which satisfies the weak maximum principle.
		
		
		(i) If  $\, 1<p<+\infty$ and $0<r<p$, then  \eqref{strong-type} holds if and only if
					\begin{equation}\label{r-p}
\int_\Omega ({G} \sigma)^{\frac{pr}{p-r}} d \sigma<+\infty.
\end{equation}
		
		
		(ii) If  $\, 0<q<1$ and $q<r < \infty$,  then there exists a positive (super) solution $u\in L^r(\Omega, d \sigma)$ to \eqref{sol} if and only if \eqref{strong-type} holds with $p=\frac{r}{q}$, or equivalently
		by statement~(i),
		\begin{equation}\label{r-q-2}
\int_\Omega ({G} \sigma)^{\frac{r}{1-q}} d \sigma<+\infty.
\end{equation}
		\end{theorem}
	
	\begin{remark}\label{rm1} {\rm We observe that the ``if'' parts of statements (i) and (ii) of Theorem~\ref{thm-a}
	 fail if $p=1$, and $r=q$, respectively. 	The ``only if'' parts hold for all $0<r<p$ in statement (i),
	 and $r>0$ in statement (ii).}
	    \end{remark}
	
	    \begin{remark}\label{rm2}
	    {\rm It is known that inequality \eqref{strong-type} with $p=\frac{r}{q}\ge 1$ in the case $0<q<1$ yields the existence of a positive (super) solution $u\in L^r(\Omega, \sigma)$ for \eqref{sol}. This statement  follows from a  lemma due to Gagliardo \cite{G}, and does not require $G$ to be quasi-symmetric or to satisfy the weak maximum principle. However, the converse statement fails without the weak maximum principle  (see  \cite{QV2} in the case $r=q$).}
	    \end{remark}
	
	 	\begin{remark}\label{rm3}  {\rm Without the assumption that $G$ satisfies the weak maximum principle,
	 the ``only if'' parts of statement (i) (with $p=\frac{r}{q}\ge 1$) and
	statement   (ii) (with $r \ge q$) of Theorem \ref{thm-a} hold only for $0<r \le 1-q^2$; see \cite{V}.
	The case $r=q$ when $0<q<q_0=\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}$  is discussed above.}
	 \end{remark}
	 	



  \section{Positive solutions in {\boldmath$W^{1, p}(\Omega)$} and {\boldmath$L^r(\Omega)$}}\label{Lpsolutions}

  	
		
	
	In this section, we present sharp conditions on the coefficient $\sigma$ which ensure the existence a positive weak solution $u \in W^{1, p}(\Omega)$ or  $u \in L^r(\Omega, \sigma)$   to  the sublinear elliptic
	Dirichlet problem
		\begin{equation}
			\label{lap_eqn}
		 \begin{cases}
			 -\text{div}  (\mathcal{A} \nabla u ) = \sigma u^q, &u>0 \, \text{ in } \Omega, \\
			u = 0 & \text{ on } \partial \Omega,
		\end{cases}
		\end{equation}
	where $0 < q < 1$, and $\sigma$ is a locally integrable function, or measure, in a domain $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ with nonnegative Green's function $G$ associated with the uniformly elliptic operator $-\text{div}  (\mathcal{A} \nabla \cdot )$, for a symmetric matrix $\mathcal{A}\in L^\infty(\Omega)^{n\times n}$.  Some of these results obtained in \cite{QV3} hold for  analogous fractional Laplacian equations \eqref{frac_lap_eqn} in domains $\Omega$ with positive Green's function.

	
	
	
	More generally,
	for a lower semicontinuous kernel
	$G\colon \Omega\times \Omega\to (0, +\infty]$,  where $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$, we denote by
	$$G (f \, d \sigma)(x) =\int_{\Omega} G(x, y) \, f(y) \, d \sigma(y), \quad x \in \Omega, $$
	 the corresponding integral operator, where  $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^+(\Omega)$, the class of
	 positive locally finite Borel measures in $\Omega$.
	
	
	
		
	We study positive solutions $u \in L^r(\Omega, d\sigma)$, as well as $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$, for the integral equation
			\begin{equation}	\label{int_eqn}
			u = G(u^q d \sigma), \quad 0<u<+\infty \quad d \sigma-\text{a.e.}  \, \,  \text{ in $\Omega$},
				\end{equation}
				where $0<q<1$.
				If $G$ is Green's kernel associated with the Laplacian in a domain
	$\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$, then \eqref{int_eqn}
	 is equivalent to  the sublinear elliptic boundary value problem    \eqref{lap_eqn} (see \cite{QV2}).
				
				
	As above, we assume that the kernel $G$ of the integral operator $G$ is lower semicontinuous, quasi-symmetric, and  satisfies a weak maximum principle. 	 In Theorem \ref{thm2}  below an additional assumption is imposed on $G$,
		\begin{equation}\label{riesz-i}
G(x, y) \le \frac{C}{|x-y|^{n-\alpha}}, \quad x, y \in \Omega,
\end{equation}
for $0<\alpha<n$; here $C$ is a positive constant which does not depend on $x, y$.
	
Such restrictions are satisfied by the Green kernel associated with many elliptic operators, including the fractional Laplacian
	$(- \Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$ and $\mathcal{A}$-Laplacian (with $\alpha=2$) in a domain $\Omega\subseteq \R^n$.
		
		The following theorems and corollaries are proved in \cite{QV3}.
			 		
				
\begin{theorem}\label{thm1}
		Let $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$, where $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ ($n \ge 1$) is a domain with a positive Green's function $G$ associated with  $-{\rm div}  (\mathcal{A} \nabla \cdot )$. Let $0<q<1$ and $\gamma>0$. Then there exists a positive solution $u$ to  \eqref{lap_eqn} such that
		\begin{equation}\label{gamma}
		\int_\Omega \vert \nabla u \vert^2 u^{\gamma-1} \, dx < \infty
	\end{equation}
	if and only if
		\begin{equation}\label{r-q}
\int_\Omega (G \sigma)^{\frac{\gamma+q}{1-q}} d \sigma<+\infty.
\end{equation}

In particular, in the case $\gamma=1$, the condition
\begin{equation}\label{fin-energy}
\int_\Omega (G \sigma)^{\frac{1+q}{1-q}} d \sigma<+\infty
\end{equation}
is necessary and sufficient for the existence of  a positive solution $u\in W^{1, 2}_0(\Omega)$ to
 \eqref{lap_eqn}.
		\end{theorem}
		
	Theorem \ref{thm1} yields sufficient conditions for $u \in W^{1, p}_0(\Omega)$ when $\frac{n}{n-1} <p<2$.  Here $W^{1, p}_0(\Omega)$ stands for
		the homogeneous Sobolev space defined as the closure of $C^\infty_0(\Omega)$ functions in the norm
		\[
	\Vert u\Vert_{W^{1, p}(\Omega)}=\Big (\int_\Omega |\nabla u|^p dx\Big)^{\frac{1}{p}}.
	\]
Letting $p=\frac{n(1+\gamma)}{n-1+\gamma}$ with $0<\gamma <1$ in
Theorem \ref{thm1}, we obtain the following corollary.
		
		
		\begin{cor}\label{cor1}  Under the assumptions of Theorem \ref{thm1}, suppose that $\frac{n}{n-1} <p<2$ and $n \ge 3$. If
		\begin{equation}\label{r-q-cor1}
\int_\Omega (G \sigma)^{\frac{p(n-2)}{(1-q)(n-p)}-1} d \sigma<+\infty,
\end{equation}
then there exists a positive solution $u \in W^{1, p}_0(\Omega)$ to  \eqref{lap_eqn}.
		\end{cor}
		
		
\begin{cor}\label{cor2}  Under the assumptions of Theorem \ref{thm1}, suppose  $\sigma \in L^s (\Omega)$, where $\frac{n}{n-q(n-2)}<s\le \frac{2n}{n+2-q(n-2)}$. Then there exists a positive solution $u \in
  W^{1, p}_0(\Omega)  \cap L^r(\Omega)$ to \eqref{lap_eqn} with $p=\frac{n(1-q)s}{n-s(1+q)}$
and $r=\frac{n(1-q)s}{n-2s}$.
		\end{cor}
				
		
		In the following theorem we give conditions for the existence of positive
		solutions $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$ for more general kernels $G$.
		
\begin{theorem}\label{thm2}  Let $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ ($n \ge 1$), and $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^{+}(\Omega)$. Let $0<q<1$.  Suppose $G$ is a positive, lower semicontinuous kernel on $\Omega \times \Omega$ which is quasi-symmetric and satisfies the weak maximum principle.



		
		
		(i) If  there exists a solution $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$, $r>0$, to \eqref{int_eqn}, then
		\begin{equation}\label{r-q-dx}
\int_\Omega (G \sigma)^{\frac{r}{1-q}} d x<+\infty.
\end{equation}

(ii) Suppose additionally that $G$ satisfies condition  \eqref{riesz-i}  for some  $0<\alpha<n$.
If  $\frac{n}{n-\alpha}<r<\infty$ and
	\begin{equation}\label{r-q-ds}
\int_\Omega (G \sigma)^{\frac{r(n-\alpha)}{(1-q)n}-1} d \sigma<+\infty,
\end{equation}
then there exists a positive solution $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$ to \eqref{int_eqn}.
		\end{theorem}



\begin{cor}\label{cor3}  Under the assumptions of Theorem \ref{thm2}, suppose that
$\sigma \in L^s (\Omega, dx)$, where $\frac{n}{n -q(n-\alpha)}<s<\frac{n}{\alpha}$. Then there exists a positive solution $u \in L^r(\Omega, dx)$ to \eqref{int_eqn}, where  $r=\frac{n(1-q)s}{n-\alpha s}$.
		\end{cor}
		


		
		Corollaries \ref{cor2} and \ref{cor3} for bounded domains $\Omega$ 	were obtained earlier by Boccardo and Orsina \cite{BO}. We observe that the restrictions on $s$ in these corollaries, and consequently on the exponents $\gamma$, $p$ and $r$
	in 	Theorem \ref{thm1}, Theorem \ref{thm2}, and Corollary \ref{cor1}, are sharp.
	
	
	
	
	There are also weak-type
	analogues of these results for Marcinkiewicz spaces
	$L^{r, \infty}$ as in \cite{BO2}, based on the corresponding weak-type weighted norm inequalities (see  \cite{QV1,QV2,V}).
		
		The proof of Theorem \ref{thm1} in \cite{QV3}
makes use of   the weighted norm inequality
		\begin{equation}\label{weight-1}
\Vert G (f \, d \sigma)\Vert_{L^r(\Omega, d \sigma)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma)}, \quad \forall f \in L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma),
\end{equation}
for integral operators $G$  in the case $1<r<s<+\infty$ considered in Sec. \ref{upper}.
		
The proof of Theorem \ref{thm2}
 uses  both \eqref{weight-1} and
a similar weighted norm inequality with Lebesgue measure on the left-hand side,
\begin{equation}\label{weight-2}
\Vert G (f \, d \sigma)\Vert_{L^r(\Omega, dx)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma)}, \quad \forall f \in L^{s} (\Omega, d \sigma),
\end{equation}
 for $1<r<\infty$, $1<s<+\infty$.  Such inequalities in the equivalent dual form
 \begin{equation}\label{weight-dual}
\Vert G f \Vert_{L^{s'}(\Omega, d\sigma)} \le C \, \Vert f \Vert_{L^{r'} (\Omega, d x)}, \quad \forall f \in L^{r'} (\Omega, d x),
\end{equation}
were characterized  in the special case where
$\Omega=\R^n$ and $G=(-\Delta)^{-\frac{\alpha}{2}}$ is the Riesz potential of order $\alpha\in (0, n)$, by  Maz'ya for $r \ge s\ge 1$, and Maz'ya and Netrusov for $1<r < s$,  in terms of Sobolev capacities, and
later by Cascante, Ortega and the author in terms of Wolff potentials (see \cite{Maz,COV}).

In \cite{QV3}, we employ an
alternative characterization in terms of Green's potentials $G \sigma$ as in Theorem \ref{thm-a} above, which is sufficient for both
\eqref{weight-1} and \eqref{weight-2}  simultaneously.

\begin{summary} \!\!В работе представлен обзор последних результатов о поло\-жительных решениях эллиптических уравнений типа
$-Lu+ V \,  u^{q}=f$, где $L$~--- эллиптический оператор в дивергентной форме, $0<q<1$, $f\geq 0$ и $V$~--- функция, которая может изменять знак, в области $\Omega \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}$ или на весовом римановом многообразии с положительной функцией Грина $G$. Обсуждаются вопросы существования решений, глобальные нижние и верхние поточечные оценки классических и слабых решений $u$, а также условия, обеспечивающие
$u \in L^r(\Omega)$ или $u \in W^{1, p} (\Omega)$.

Некоторые из этих результатов применимы к однородным сублинейным интегральным уравнениям $ u = G(u^q d \sigma)$  in $\Omega,$
	где $0<q<1$, а $\sigma=-V$ --- положительная локально конечная борелевская мера в $\Omega$. Здесь ${G} (f \, d \sigma)(x) =\int_\Omega G(x, y), \, f(y) \, d \sigma(y)$ --- интегральный оператор с положительным (квази) симметричным ядром $G$ на $\Omega \times \Omega$, который удовлетворяет слабому принципу максимума. Результаты распространяются на положительные, возможно сингулярные, решения сублинейных уравнений, содержащих дробный лапласиан
		\[ (-\Delta)^{\frac{\alpha}{2}} u = \sigma \, u^q, \quad u \ge 0  \quad \text{в} \, \, \Omega, \]
	где  $0<q<1$, $0 < \alpha < n$ и $u=0$ в $\Omega^c$ и на бесконечности в областях  $\Omega \subseteq \R^n$ с положительной функцией Грина $G$.
\end{summary}
